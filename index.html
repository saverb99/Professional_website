About Me

I’m a biomedical engineer with 6+ years of experience developing and testing next-generation medical technologies that interface seamlessly with the human body. My work centers on designing, fabricating, and validating high-performance bioelectronic systems - ranging from neural interfaces to implantable sensors - that enable precise recording and modulation of physiological signals. While my recent focus has been in neural engineering, including electrocorticography (ECoG) arrays and stimulation platforms, the skills I’ve built extend across all physiological domains such as cardiac pacing, glucose sensing, BCI integration, and auditory prosthetics. I specialize in soft materials integration, electrochemical device characterization (EIS, CV, VT), electrophysiology, photolithography, and additive manufacturing, and I’ve led projects from early prototyping through in vivo validation. By leveraging direct-ink-write printing and other rapid fabrication techniques, I aim to shorten development timelines and create customizable,

scalable platforms suited for both clinical and research applications. My interdisciplinary background bridges bioengineering, materials science, and electrical design, allowing me to approach complex medical problems with a systems-level perspective. Outside the lab, I’m an avid distance runner and enjoy the time outside that comes from training for marathons. I also love hiking new trails, biking through the city, reading fantasy fiction novels, experimenting in the kitchen, and spending time with my dog Lizzy - especially on long walks through the neighborhoods of Philadelphia.
